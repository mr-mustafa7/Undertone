# ReimagineHealth — eMed x OpenAI Hackathon

**Event:** ReimagineHealth (AI Engine x eMed)
**Location:** London, England
**Kickoff:** Friday 7pm → Submissions due Saturday 3pm
**Team size:** Up to 4
**Backed by:** OpenAI, Eka Ventures, Dawn Capital, British Land

## The Challenge

Chronic conditions (obesity, type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease) affect millions of
people, yet most healthcare systems still treat them reactively and in-clinic. eMed has
spent years proving that the opposite is possible: that patients managed at home, with the
right tools, can achieve outcomes far beyond what traditional care delivers. eMed sees 90%
adherence rates (keeping to the recommended programme of medication and/or behaviour) where
the industry averages 40–60%.

**The question:** what does the next generation of at-home chronic condition management
look like, powered by AI?

Not looking for incremental improvements to existing apps. Genuinely novel ideas: new ways
to support patients through long programmes, surfaces for monitoring and coaching that feel
human, tools that make adherence effortless, or entirely new ways to think about how AI can
sit alongside someone managing a long-term health condition.

## What to Build

An AI-powered tool or product concept for at-home (or non-clinic-based) chronic condition
management. Example challenge areas (not exhaustive):

- Inadequate at-home biomarker monitoring tools lead to immature data interpretation and
  missed critical signals.
- How can we create AI that offers genuine, ongoing support for long-term health?
- How do we provide better support for patients managing complex needs like heart or
  women's health?
- How can we connect patients with their clinical teams without needing constant,
  real-time appointments?
- How can we turn complex health data into simple, actionable advice for patients?

## Judging Criteria

- **User impact** — Does this genuinely improve life for a patient managing a chronic
  condition? Is the need real and the solution meaningfully better than what exists?
- **Innovation** — Is this a novel approach? Ideas that couldn't have existed two years
  ago, or that reframe the problem in a surprising way.
- **Feasibility** — Could this be built and deployed in the real world?
- **Demo quality** — Can you show it working, even in prototype form? A live demo or
  convincing walkthrough matters more than slides.
