# Platform Names — Enterprise Medtech

The name has to pass three tests: (1) a cardiologist would say it without smirking, (2) a VC would fund a company called it, (3) it's ownable (not already a voice-health company). Avoid the taken ones: **Vocalis, Canary (Speech), Auris (J&J), Lumen, Baseline (Verily)** are all real health companies — don't collide.

## Top tier (recommended)

| Name | Why it works | Etymology / hook | Caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Auscura** ⭐ | Sounds like a real diagnostics company; unique, ownable, clinically deep | *Auscultation* (the medical act of listening to the body) + *cura* (care). Literally "listening-care." | Coined — check .com / TM, likely clear |
| **Precede** ⭐ | The entire thesis *is* the name: the signal precedes the symptom | "We see it before it happens" | Common word — pair as "Precede Health" |
| **Timbre** | Short, memorable, and it's the *exact* acoustic property that changes with disease | The quality of a sound that carries the health signal | Real word — "Timbre Health" for TM room |
| **Sonus** | Clean, Latin, clinical — reads like an established device company | Latin *sonus* = sound | Some small cos exist; "Sonus Bio/Health" |

## Strong alternates

| Name | Angle |
|---|---|
| **Attune** | Attunement / listening closely — warm but professional; "attuned to your body" |
| **Resona** | Resonance of the voice; modern, ownable |
| **Undertone** | The current build name — "listening beneath the surface"; solid, evocative |
| **Cadence** | Strategist's pick — rhythm/trajectory (note: a large fintech uses it; some collision) |
| **Prosody** | The literal acoustic-feature term — insider-clever, but abstract to laypeople |
| **Sentinel** | Watchtower / continuous monitoring — strong but generic in security |

## Recommendation
Lead with **Auscura** (most ownable + most credibly medical) or **Precede** (best narrative — the name argues your whole thesis). Both survive a clinical room and a VC room. Keep **Undertone** as the fallback since the demo already carries it.

> Before committing: check `[name].com`, `[name].health`, and a quick USPTO/EUIPO trademark search — takes 5 minutes and saves a rename later.
