Create a 6-slide investor/judge pitch deck for a healthtech startup called "Auscura" (working name — a voice-based AI platform). Audience: clinical judges, healthcare investors (Eka Ventures, Dawn Capital), and OpenAI, at the ReimagineHealth hackathon. Tone: confident, evidence-driven, aspirational but credible. Visual style: dark enterprise medical dashboard — near-black navy background, teal (#22c197) and soft-red (#f0556b) accents, clean sans-serif, generous spacing, data-forward. Every claim below is real and cited — keep the citations visible as small source lines.

SLIDE 1 — The silent gap
Headline: "Chronic disease is won or lost between appointments."
Body: 50% of patients on long-term metabolic programmes quit within a year. Medication non-adherence causes ~$100–300B in avoidable US spend annually and is linked to 200,000 deaths a year in Europe. The one thing proven to keep patients on track is frequent contact — patients with 5+ visits are 49× more likely to stay on therapy at 120 days — but the system can't scale it: only ~9,900 obesity specialists in the US, with 3–5 year waitlists. The dangerous silence is the space between visits.
Visual: a flat clinical timeline with a long empty gap between two appointment dots; the gap glowing red.

SLIDE 2 — The insight: your voice is a vital sign
Headline: "The body speaks before the patient does."
Body: Fluid, muscle loss, and metabolic change physically alter how the voice sounds — days to weeks before a patient notices. This is proven science: Cordio's HearO detects worsening heart failure 3 weeks early at 76–81% sensitivity across 545 patients and 660,000 recordings — versus just ~20% for daily-weight monitoring. Peer-reviewed 2024–25 studies (JMIR) show voice detects sarcopenia and frailty; hydration and glycemic voice signals are published too. Why now: real-time voice AI and multimodal models only crossed the line in the last 18 months.
Visual: a waveform morphing into a clean rising trend line; small callout "76–81% sensitivity, 3 weeks early."

SLIDE 3 — The product: a listening layer
Headline: "Auscura — the listening layer for chronic disease."
Body: Patients do a 60-second daily voice check-in with an AI companion — no wearable, no extra task, no app to remember. Underneath the conversation, Auscura extracts acoustic biomarkers and tracks drift per organ system. When drift crosses a threshold, an explainable rule-based engine escalates a cited, structured summary to the clinician — human-in-the-loop, never an autonomous diagnosis. Three-beat product: (1) Talk — the daily check-in, (2) Snap — photo-log a reading with vision AI, (3) Hear + Escalate — the acoustic signal catches what the patient can't feel, and flags it. Include a line: "Live demo: it caught early muscle loss from a phone call — weeks before a DEXA scan would."
Visual: a clean three-step flow: microphone → waveform-to-trend → clinician alert card.

SLIDE 4 — Why it's defensible
Headline: "Proven science. Novel application. Real moat."
Body: We didn't invent the acoustic science — we point it where no one else has. HearO owns already-diagnosed heart-failure patients; we screen the far larger metabolic population who get zero cardiac or muscle monitoring today. Three defensibility pillars: (1) Distribution — the biomarker rides inside a daily conversation that already has to happen for adherence, solving the engagement collapse that kills point-solution apps (only ~2% of health apps see sustained use). (2) Trust — every escalation is an explainable, cited reasoning trace, not a black-box score, which is the #1 barrier to clinical AI adoption. (3) Compounding data — longitudinal voice + outcome data per patient becomes a moat that deepens the longer it runs (cf. Twin Health, $53M on validated data). Regulatory posture: triage-to-human, not a medical device — deployable now.
Visual: a 2x2 or three-pillar layout; small competitor note "vs. HearO: different population, passive capture, multi-organ."

SLIDE 5 — Market & the business
Headline: "A vital sign for a $100B+ metabolic market."
Body: The GLP-1/obesity category is a $100B market with $2.8B already raised by venture-backed players; the voice-biomarker market grows from ~$1.1B (2024) to $5.4B (2035). The buyer already exists and is already losing money: payers, employers, and GLP-1 providers all bleed spend on the 50% who quit — an adherence lift has direct, hard-dollar ROI, so we sell into an existing budget line, not a new one. Land in metabolic/GLP-1 programmes; expand across the disease trajectory.
Visual: a market-size step chart ($1.1B → $5.4B) beside a simple "who pays" row (payers · employers · providers).

SLIDE 6 — The 3-year vision
Headline: "In three years, the annual check-up disappears."
Body: One listening layer, four organ systems: muscle is live today; cardiac, glycemic, and renal are on the roadmap — added by writing new rules, not retraining a black box. Disease stops being caught at a scheduled appointment once a year and starts being caught the Tuesday it begins, because the patient's voice already told us — before they knew themselves. Voice becomes the free, continuous vital sign that sits inside every call a chronic-disease patient already makes. Close with: "We built the muscle detector live this weekend to prove it works. This is what AI in medicine looks like in 2029."
Visual: one central "voice" node radiating to four organ icons (muscle highlighted, three roadmap); an aspirational, forward horizon feel.

For every slide, keep text tight and scannable (headline + 3–4 short supporting points), put numbers in bold, and render source citations as small muted footnote lines (e.g., "Cordio HearO, AHA 2023 · JMIR 2024 · AJMC"). Do not invent statistics beyond those given.
